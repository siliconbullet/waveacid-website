Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1. Which compounds is the synthesizer going to be most used for?
  • We assume it is cosmetics, antibiotics, immunosuppressants, anti-cancer drugs, disease prevention agents, pain killers, birth control pills and psychoactives.

  • 2. How does it work?
  • The WaveAcid Biochemical Synthesizer uses a base cartridge and advanced technology to compile pharmaceutical molecules from inorganic compounds and biochemical precursors.

  • 3. What's a base cartridge?
  • A base cartridge contains amounts of basically every chemical element and a set of biochemical precursors such as Phenol, Benzene and (R)-4-Hydroxyphenylglycin. It also contains distilled Water, and several gases like Carbondioxide. The cartridge is meant to be exchangeable when empty.

  • 4. How long does a single cartridge last?
  • Depending on the quantities and amounts that you are printing, a single cartridge might last for 1-15 pills of a substance. Numbers vary immensly between different molecules.

  • 5. What's Infrared (IR) Spectroscopy?
  • It's a technique that uses infrared radiation to excite the molecules of a compound and generates an infrared spectrum of the energy by a molecule as a function of the frequency or a wavelength of light. For example, O-H bonds are stronger than C-H bonds. The measurable result is that O-H bonds vibrate at higher frequencies than C-H bonds. IR Spectroscopy is a method of traditional analytical chemistry and data generated by it generally gets displayed after or during mathematical operations such as Fourier Transform. We use it in our device for intermediate step verification, amongside Nuclear Magnet Resonance.

  • 6. How do you go from chemical elements to inorganic compounds to biochemical precursors?
  • Substantial amounts of heat are generated by lasers in small chambers. Sequentially executed rounds of chemical reactions lead by an NVIDIA GPU cluster produce inorganic compounds. Single-atom and subatomic manipulation methods were borrowed from recent advances in Quantum Computing.

  • 12. What's High-performance liquid chromatography? (HPLC)
  • It's a technique in analytical chemistry used to identify and seperate components of a mixture, including stereoisomerically chiral components. It uses a moving gas stream of Helium or Nitrogen to carry a sample.

  • 13. What's Nuclear Magnet Resonance (NMR)?
  • It's a technique that uses a strong magnet field measured in 'Tesla', shielded from any outside disturbances, for whole molecule identification. It does so by harnessing quantum effects of charged atomic nuclei, referred to as spin.

  • 14. What's a Flow-Chemical Microreactor?
  • It's a very small platine, similiar in size and design to a silicon processor (sometimes even using silicon), that instead of electronic circutis, implements chemical circuits, for example to accomplish Friedel-Craft acylation.

  • 15. What's Electrolysis?
  • In chemistry and drug synthesis, it's a technique that uses a direct electric current to drive an otherwise non-spontaneous chemical reaction.

  • 16. What's an Intermediate Step or Intermediate Product?
  • Different pharmaceutical or chemical compounds require, depending on the base cartridge and precursors used, a varying number of intermediate synthesis steps. That is different chemical compounds that occur in the process, before the final product was finished.

  • 17. How do you assure purity for human consumption?
  • We are using a Palm-Size NMR and are developing a very small implementation of Inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry (ICP-AES) to make sure there is not even traces of potentially toxic or harmful substances or materials in the intermediate steps or final synthesis product. While the device is still in the work, we are using state-of-the-art HPLC, NMR, IR Spectrometers and a Nanometer Resolution Microscope.

  • 20. What's the projected run-time for a single pill print?

  • 24. What if the side products of chemical reactions are harmful or toxic?
  • Garbage collection and decomposition is constantly in progress. Disposable outputs from the device are completely harmless, while intermediate steps inside the chambers of course are not. The isolated tank structure and outer vacuum-seperated metal shell of the device makes sure that no toxic compounds ever exit the device. Any toxic compounds that are not needed for further chemical reactions are going to be instantly fragmented into chemical elements.

  • The WaveAcid Biochemical Synthesizer is both dealing with mixing ratios, temperature, light and pressure; and is also manipulating molecules on a subatomic level.
